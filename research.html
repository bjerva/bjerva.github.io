Research

Studying language models through linguistic structure.

My research examines how linguistic structure can help us understand and evaluate language models. It spans multilingual NLP, language-model security and privacy, linguistic typology, and formal and computational semantics.

01

Language-model security

Linguistic perspectives on language-model security.

I study security and privacy risks in language models and text embeddings, including inversion attacks, information leakage, poisoning, and manipulated or machine-generated text. A central question is how these risks and possible defences vary across languages.

Information leakage

When can embeddings or model outputs be inverted to recover sensitive source text, and how do these risks vary across languages?

Poisoning and manipulation

How do linguistic properties interact with poisoning, model confusion, and the detection of manipulated or machine-generated text?

Evaluation in practice

How should security and ethics be evaluated under real deployment conditions rather than only through benchmark averages?

02

Multilingual NLP

Multilingual does not automatically mean inclusive.

Research and evaluation in multilingual NLP remain concentrated in a relatively small number of high-resource languages. My work uses linguistic typology to improve models, datasets, and evaluation practices across a broader range of languages.

Typological grounding

What do language representations learn about phonology, morphology, syntax, and semantics—and when does that knowledge support transfer?

Representative evaluation

Which language samples support defensible claims about multilingual generalisation, and what does meaningful diversity require?

Low-resource languages

How can data quality, targeted adaptation, and linguistic knowledge improve outcomes where digital resources are scarce?

03

Formal and computational semantics

Linguistic structure can make model behaviour easier to analyse.

My work draws on formal and computational semantics to investigate how models represent meaning, inference, and context—and how linguistic methods can support better evaluation and interpretability.

Semantic representation

What aspects of meaning and inference are captured by current language models, and where do their representations remain inadequate?

Linguistic analysis

How can syntax, morphology, semantics, and discourse help explain model behaviour beyond aggregate performance scores?

Safety and reliability

How can linguistically grounded analysis support sharper evaluation of model reliability, interpretability, and security?
