Teaching & supervision

Learning by connecting foundations to practice.

I teach natural language processing and artificial intelligence at Aalborg University, with an emphasis on active learning, technical depth, and responsible system building.

Approach

Students should leave able to reason—not merely reproduce.

My teaching connects linguistic and computational foundations to the design and evaluation of real systems. Students work with contemporary methods while learning to question assumptions, recognise limitations, and communicate evidence precisely.

I was selected by students as Teacher of the Year at the Department of Computer Science in 2021.

Supervision

Independence with an active intellectual partnership.

I supervise PhD, MSc, and BSc researchers across NLP, multilinguality, language-model security, semantics, and factuality. My aim is to combine clear expectations and regular feedback with growing ownership of the research direction.

Prospective PhD candidates should normally apply through advertised positions. For thesis collaboration or research visits, a concise message describing the question and fit is welcome.
